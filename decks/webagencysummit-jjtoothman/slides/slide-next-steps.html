Some next steps

Two things worth doing this quarter.

01
Write the job description for the AI hire you'd most want to make.
Not a prompt. The job — title, role, responsibilities, systems they'd touch, the kind of person they'd be. You'll learn more from that exercise than from a year of prompt-tuning.
02
Get fluent in the differences between AI models.
OpenClaw has the concept of subagents — you can assign specific models to specific tasks. Don't burn frontier-model tokens on something that's just checking CPU health or disk space. The agency that knows when to use which model spends a lot less.
