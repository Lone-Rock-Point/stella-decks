What I'd tell you

Five things I didn't expect.

Discovery one
Personification makes a big difference.
Viv has a background. A taste. A way of writing. Inside OpenClaw, this lives in SOUL.md — durable identity, not a prompt I hope she remembers.
Discovery two
Always-on beats session-based.
Viv isn't a chat window I open. She's running. The morning briefing arrives because she's been working overnight — not because I prompted her.
Discovery three
Centralization beats individual setups.
One workspace. One source of truth for skills, prompts, integrations. The team isn't running 12 versions of Claude with 12 prompt libraries. We're all using the same Viv.
Discovery four
The gateway routes the models, not me.
I don't switch between Claude, OpenAI, and local models. OpenClaw routes whichever model fits the task. I just talk to Viv.
Discovery five
It can be divisive.
Not everyone agrees with relating to AI in human terms. Naming it, giving it a personality, treating it like a colleague — some people find that useful, some find it uncomfortable. Worth knowing going in.
