What I wanted

An executive assistant. A chief of staff.

I've tried this before.
Three virtual assistants over the years — none stuck. Onboarding overhead never paid back the time saved.
A freelance chief of staff.
Better. Smart, capable, expensive. But the relationship was bounded by hours-per-week, and the company wasn't hers to obsess over.
What I kept landing on.
I needed someone embedded in the company, paying attention all day, owning a real scope of work. The economics of that as a human role never quite penciled out for me.
